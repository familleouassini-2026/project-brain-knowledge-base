## Vision

We believe that the true value of a software product does not live in its code.

It lives in the knowledge that gave birth to that code.

Technologies evolve.

Frameworks change.

Languages appear and disappear.

Artificial intelligence systems improve and are replaced.

The code itself may be rewritten several times during the lifetime of a product.

But the knowledge, decisions, business vision and rules that govern a system must survive time.

Project Brain is born from this conviction.

Its ambition is not to create yet another development platform.

Its ambition is to preserve the knowledge of a project so that it can be understood, transmitted, enriched and reused independently from the technologies used to implement it.

We believe that good architecture must be understandable before it is developed.

We believe that a project must be transferable to another team, another company or another artificial intelligence without losing its identity.

We believe that artificial intelligence must support human reasoning, never replace it.

Project Brain does not aim to decide on behalf of the people who build projects.

It provides a framework, a memory, a method and an analysis capability that help people make better decisions.

Our objective is to create a durable knowledge asset.

An asset that survives tools, technologies and even its original authors.

Because software can be rewritten.

Knowledge should never be lost.
