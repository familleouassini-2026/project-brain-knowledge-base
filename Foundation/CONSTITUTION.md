## Purpose

This document defines the non-negotiable principles that govern Project Brain.

## Article 1 — Knowledge is the asset

The main asset of the project is not the code.

The main asset is the structured knowledge that explains why the code exists.

## Article 2 — Code is an implementation

Code is important, but it is not the ultimate source of truth.

It must be possible to understand the system before reading the code.

## Article 3 — Documentation comes before durable development

Any major feature, module or architectural change must be documented before being implemented.

## Article 4 — AI proposes, humans decide

Artificial intelligence can analyse, suggest, explain, draft and prepare.

It must not make irreversible decisions without human validation.

## Article 5 — No hidden architecture

Architecture must never exist only in a conversation, in one person's head or in code structure.

It must be documented, versioned and explainable.

## Article 6 — No major change without impact analysis

Any major change must describe:

- what changes;
- why it changes;
- what is impacted;
- what risks are introduced;
- what alternatives were considered;
- how the change can be tested.

## Article 7 — The smallest compatible change is preferred

The project must avoid unnecessary complexity.

When several solutions exist, the preferred solution is the smallest one that preserves the long-term architecture.

## Article 8 — The Product Owner must not carry technical complexity

The Product Owner expresses business needs and makes decisions.

Project Brain and the architecture process must translate those needs into technical impact analysis.

## Article 9 — The system must survive its authors

The project must be understandable and maintainable by people or AI systems that were not present at its creation.

## Article 10 — Every release must leave a concrete artifact

A work session is valuable only if it produces a concrete artifact:

- a document;
- a decision;
- a release;
- a prototype;
- a tested feature;
- a validated correction.

## Article 11 — Trust is built through deliverables

The quality of the project is measured by what is delivered, not by what is promised.

## Article 12 — The constitution can evolve only by amendment

This constitution is stable but not frozen.

Any future change must be handled through an explicit amendment with a reason, an impact and a version update.
